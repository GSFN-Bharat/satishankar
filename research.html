Research Programme

A long-duration investigation into the structural, ontological, and epistemic foundations of Vedic knowledge systems and their dialogue with contemporary scientific and philosophical inquiry.

Central Thesis

Vedic knowledge systems encode structured ontological and cognitive frameworks that are formally analyzable and capable of systematic dialogue with modern science, mathematics, and philosophy.

Generative Framework

The research is organized around a recursive generative cycle:

Astitva → Vedic-Hṛdaya-Cakṣu → Svagarbhā → Astitva

This cycle represents a self-referential structure of emergence, cognition, and re-articulation across ontological and epistemic domains.

Core Orientation

The work proceeds through close textual engagement with the Ṛg Veda, Brāhmaṇa literature, and classical darśanic traditions, combined with formal analytical reasoning and interdisciplinary synthesis.

Primary Domains

  • Vedic Literature (Ṛg Veda, Brāhmaṇas, Upaniṣads)
  • Indian Philosophical Systems (Nyāya, Vaiśeṣika, Sāṁkhya, Vedānta)
  • Philosophy of Science
  • Mathematical and Formal Structures
  • Cognitive and Consciousness Studies

Primary Textual Base

  • Ṛg Veda Saṁhitā
  • Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa
  • Upaniṣadic corpus
  • Pāṇinian grammatical tradition

Key Conceptual Contributions

Vedic Exemplarism

A foundational interpretative framework in which Vedic entities are understood as generative exemplars structuring both cosmological processes and cognitive operations. It enables a unified reading of nāma–rūpa relations across textual, ontological, and epistemological domains.

Agni–Manas Structure

A model analyzing the relationship between cognitive processes (manas) and energetic-intentional structures (Agni), integrating Vedic insights with contemporary perspectives on cognition and systems theory.

Ṛta as Ontological Order

Ṛta is interpreted as intrinsic structural law governing manifestation, cognition, and transformation—extending beyond the notion of cosmic order into a universal organizing principle.

Svagarbhā Framework

A conceptual model describing self-contained generative systems, offering insight into recursive structures in Vedic cosmology and cognition.

Research Methodology

  • Textual analysis of primary Vedic sources
  • Comparative philosophical interpretation
  • Formal and mathematical modeling
  • Interdisciplinary synthesis

Integration with Scholarly System

The research programme is operationalized through the Astitva Archive, with materials preserved on Zenodo and disseminated under persistent identifiers for global accessibility.
