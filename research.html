Research Programme

Central Thesis

The research programme advances the thesis that Vedic knowledge systems encode structured ontological and cognitive frameworks that are formally analyzable and capable of dialogue with contemporary scientific and mathematical thought.

The research programme represents a long-duration investigation into the structural, ontological, and epistemic foundations of Vedic knowledge systems and their relevance to contemporary scientific and philosophical inquiry.

Generative Framework

The research is organized around a recursive generative cycle:

Astitva → Vedic-Hṛdaya-Cakṣu → Svagarbhā → Astitva

This cycle represents a self-referential structure of emergence, cognition, and re-articulation across ontological and epistemic domains.

Core Orientation

The work proceeds through close textual engagement with the Ṛg Veda, Brāhmaṇa literature, and classical darśanic traditions, combined with formal analytical reasoning and interdisciplinary synthesis.

Primary Domains

  • Vedic Literature (Ṛg Veda, Brāhmaṇas, Upaniṣads)
  • Indian Philosophical Systems (Nyāya, Vaiśeṣika, Sāṁkhya, Vedānta)
  • Philosophy of Science
  • Mathematical and Formal Structures
  • Cognitive and Consciousness Studies

Key Conceptual Contributions

Vedic Exemplarism

A framework interpreting Vedic entities as archetypal structures that generate and regulate manifest reality across multiple domains of existence.

Vedic Exemplarism

A foundational interpretative framework in which Vedic entities are understood not as symbolic constructs but as generative exemplars that structure both cosmological processes and cognitive operations. This framework enables a unified reading of nāma–rūpa relations across textual, ontological, and epistemological domains.

Agni–Manas Structure

A model examining the relationship between cognitive processes (manas) and energetic-intentional structures (Agni) as described in Vedic texts.

Ṛta as Ontological Order

Interpretation of Ṛta not merely as cosmic order but as intrinsic structural law governing manifestation, cognition, and transformation.

Svagarbhā Framework

A conceptual model describing self-contained generative systems in Vedic cosmology and cognition.

Research Methodology

  • Textual analysis of primary Vedic sources
  • Comparative philosophical interpretation
  • Formal and mathematical modeling
  • Interdisciplinary synthesis

Integration with Scholarly System

The research programme is operationalized through the Astitva Archive, with materials preserved on Zenodo and disseminated under persistent identifiers for global accessibility.
